Exoplanet Naming Systems

Exoplanets, planets outside our solar system, are given names based on structured naming conventions. Two main naming systems are used:

1. IAU Designations

The International Astronomical Union (IAU) assigns names to exoplanets using the name of the star, followed by a lowercase letter. The first exoplanet discovered around a star gets the letter “b,” and subsequent discoveries are labeled with letters in the order of discovery (c, d, etc.).

Example: 51 Pegasi b is the first planet discovered around the star 51 Pegasi.

2. Catalog Designations

Many exoplanets are discovered through large surveys, so their names often include catalog numbers that indicate the mission or observatory that found them.

Common Catalogs:
  • Kepler Mission: Exoplanets discovered by the Kepler Space Telescope are named with the prefix "Kepler-" and a number for the star system, followed by a lowercase letter (e.g., Kepler-22b).
  • WISE: The WISE mission follows a similar format (e.g., WISE 0855−0714b).
  • OGLE: The OGLE project uses designations for planets discovered via gravitational lensing (e.g., OGLE-2005-BLG-390Lb).

3. Alternative Naming Systems

Occasionally, exoplanets are named through public contests or given culturally significant names.

Example: 51 Pegasi b was named Dimidium after a public voting campaign.
